Editing

I offer editing for fiction and non-fiction, bringing careful attention, respect, enthusiasm, and eight years of experience.

Types

  • Fiction: Developmental editing for narrative, characters, and pacing. Line editing for style. Copy editing for grammar and consistency.
  • Non-Fiction: Structural editing for clarity and organization. Line editing for readability and tone. Copy editing for accuracy and style.
  • Manuscript Assessment: Comprehensive and detailed feedback on strengths, improvements, and suggestions for your next draft.
  • Proofreading: Final review for typos, formatting issues, and minor errors before publication or submission.

What I Work With

I adore working with:

  • Literary fiction, speculative fiction, and experimental narratives
  • Personal essays, memoirs, and creative non-fiction
  • Zines, chapbooks, and independent publications

My Process

Every project begins with you and what'll serve you best. I provide:

  • Clear communication about scope and timeline
  • Sample edit of 1-2 pages before committing to larger projects
  • Recorded changes and comments explaining suggestions
  • Respect for your voice while strengthening clarity and impact

Pricing

Rates vary by project type and length:

  • Developmental/Structural Editing: $0.02 per word
  • Line Editing: $0.015 per word
  • Copy Editing: $0.01 per word
  • Proofreading: $0.008 per word
  • Manuscript Assessment: $200-400 depending on length

I also offer sliding scale rates. Let's discuss what works for you!

Workshops

I create engaging sessions blending practical instruction with creative exploration for art/community/homeschool/writing groups, libraries, and schools. And, perhaps, you? Every workshop I run is:

  • Adaptable to: age groups, experience levels
  • Built around active participation with adaptive and fun exercises

I have a warm, generous, and enthusiastic presence. I'm fluent in English and intermediate in Spanish.

Types

  • Drawing For Fun: Accessible collaborative prompts, games, and excersises for enjoyement and expression, regardless of skill level.
  • Creative Writing: Targeted writing prompts to spark imagination, exploring narrative, character, and dialogue. Writing as nourishment.
  • Worldbuilding: Developing fictional settings through guided exercises, exploring techniques for creating believable worlds, avoiding common worldbuilding pitfalls.
  • Zine-Making: Learning multiple zine formats and binding techinques, creating a complete mini-zine during the workshop, resources for continuing their zine practice
